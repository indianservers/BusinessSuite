# Vertical Market Analysis

Date: 2026-06-04

## Business Model Recommendation

Business Suite should be built as a modular operating system for micro, small, and medium businesses first, then expanded upward into mid-market and enterprise through industry editions, workflow governance, integration depth, and AI controls.

| Customer Size | Buy Readiness | Best Package | Sales Motion |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Micro business | Medium | Mobile-first CRM, invoicing, payroll-lite, WhatsApp workflows | Low-touch self-serve with assisted setup |
| Small business | Very high | HRMS + CRM + PMS + approvals + reports | Partner-led implementation and annual subscription |
| Medium business | Very high | Full suite with workflows, role dashboards, integrations, audit, BI | Consultative sale with vertical templates |
| Enterprise | Selective | Industry edition plus security, custom workflows, API, SSO, audit, data residency | Solution architecture-led transformation |

## Ideal First Buyers

The strongest near-term ICP is an Indian small or medium business with 50-1000 employees, distributed operations, manual approvals, Excel-heavy reporting, and disconnected HR, sales, and delivery processes. They value local compliance, fast implementation, WhatsApp/mobile workflows, and one vendor more than deep global enterprise customization.

## Vertical Entry Strategy

| Phase | Verticals | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Service companies, IT companies, CA/tax/audit firms, trading | Strong fit with current CRM/PMS/HRMS and fastest revenue conversion |
| Phase 2 | Schools, colleges, clinics, distribution | Need vertical workflows but can reuse core platform heavily |
| Phase 3 | Hospitals, manufacturing, retail, real estate | Need deeper domain integrations and compliance |
| Phase 4 | Construction | High value but requires mature project controls and cost systems |
